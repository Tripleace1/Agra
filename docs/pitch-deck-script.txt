SLIDE 4 — WHAT AGRA DOES                                     1:45 – 2:30  (45s)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  "Agra ships three offline capabilities in a single portal.

   First — SOIL DIAGNOSIS.  You enter the NPK and pH values from a soil
   test.  Agra cross-references twelve crop tolerance profiles and gives
   you a ranked match plus specific nutrient corrections.

   Second — a RAG CHAT ASSISTANT.  Ask anything about pests, irrigation,
   crop rotation.  In English or in Yorùbá.

   Third — a TRACEABILITY LEDGER.  Register a harvest batch.  Get a
   deterministic local trace ID, hashed from farmer, location, crop and
   date.

   These are not three apps stitched together.  They share a single
   SQLite database.  The soil result feeds the chat.  The chat can
   explain a registered batch's crop.  One integrated system."

[Click.]

 STAGE COACHING NOTES

  TIMING
    -  Practice with a stopwatch.  If you run over 7:30, cut slide 5 video
       to 45 seconds (the most compressible slot).
    -  Slow down on the NUMBERS slide (9).  Judges are reading; let them.

  VOICE
    -  Drop pitch on the slide title — sounds confident.
    -  Speed up the persona names (Juma / Amina / Moussa) but slow on the
       quotes.
    -  Pause one full second after every "Third" or "And third" so the
       count lands.

  BODY
    -  Hands open until slide 6, where you turn quarter-towards the screen
       and gesture at the architecture diagram.
    -  Walk one step left between slides 3 and 4 to break visual monotony.
    -  Do NOT pace.  One step, then stop.

  Q & A PREP — most likely questions
    1. "Why Qwen, not Phi-3 or Gemma?"
         Multilingual perf on Yorùbá.  Q4_K_M GGUF available.  Apache 2.0.
    2. "How do you handle hallucination on dosage recommendations?"
         Soil engine is deterministic, not LLM.  LLM only paraphrases.
    3. "What stops the user from going online once installed?"
         Nothing — but our scoring claims and verification harness assume
         offline.  We provide a ufw rule that enforces it at the kernel.
    4. "Is the Yorùbá fluent?"
         Honest answer: passable, not native.  We compensate with
         template strings for high-stakes UI text.
    5. "What's the unit economics if you commercialise?"
         Zero per-call cost.  Distribution is the only cost.  USB-stick
         kits at <$2 per farmer, recouped via cooperative subscription.

  IF THE PROJECTOR FAILS

       Do not skip slide 9 (numbers) under any circumstances.

  IF THE LIVE DEMO LAPTOP FAILS
    -  Switch immediately to the embedded video on slide 5.  Do not try
       to recover the laptop on stage; you will lose 90 seconds.
